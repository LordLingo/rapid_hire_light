# Rapid Hire — Competitive Gap Analysis & Recommendations

## Top 5 audited

I benchmarked rapidhiresolutions.com against the five companies that consistently rank at the top of US employer-side background-check lists in 2025–2026 — **First Advantage**, **Sterling** (now part of First Advantage), **Checkr**, **HireRight**, and **GoodHire** [1] [2] [3]. IBISWorld lists First Advantage as the largest US background-check business by share, and these five together cover nearly every "best for enterprise / best for SMB / best for tech" slot across the major review sites [4] [3].

## Prioritized recommendations

The recommendations below are ordered by impact-per-effort. Each one names what is missing, what the top 5 do, and the specific Rapid Hire change.

### Tier 1 — Conversion-critical, ship within the next two checkpoints

**1. Per-check service detail pages.** Every top-5 competitor exposes individual service pages — Checkr lists Engage / Verify / Decide product families with deep pages per check, HireRight publishes "150+ specific service offerings," First Advantage and Sterling categorize by check type and by audience (SMB / Mid / Enterprise / Global). Rapid Hire's `/services` page is a single bundled route. The competitive move is a `/services/<check>` URL family covering at minimum: criminal (county / national / federal), SSN trace, employment & education verification, MVR, drug screen panels, OIG/SAM exclusion, credit, civil, identity verification. Each page should carry: what the check is, what it does and does not catch, typical turnaround, FCRA permissible-purpose note, price band, and a "compare in package" link to the calculator. This is the single biggest SEO and conversion gap.

**2. Sample report download.** The Home hero already has a "View Sample Report" CTA but no artifact behind it. Every top-5 competitor offers a real sample. Build a branded, watermarked PDF (or interactive HTML) that mirrors what a real Rapid Hire report looks like — cover page, candidate identity panel, each component section with sample findings, adjudication recommendation, and an FCRA disclosure footer. Gate it lightly (email-only, no phone) so it doubles as a lead magnet.

**3. Candidate self-service: status check + FAQ.** All five competitors expose a candidate-facing status page (Profile Advantage, Candidate Hub, Checkr Profiles, Applicant Center). Rapid Hire has none. Build `/candidates` with: (a) "Check my background check status" form, (b) candidate FAQ ("what is this report? what are my rights? how do I dispute?"), (c) Spanish-language toggle. Even if status check is "contact your employer's HR" for now, the surface area is what candidates expect to find. Checkr explicitly lists "multilingual FCRA-certified support teams" as a differentiator — a Spanish FAQ is the lowest-effort win.

**4. Customer case studies + named-logo wall with proof points.** Rapid Hire has client logos in the hero but no case studies. Every top-5 has them. Author 3 anonymized-but-specific case studies (high-volume retail, healthcare credentialing, transportation MVR) modeled on the existing white paper voice — situation, screening program design, measurable outcome (turnaround, dispute rate, audit pass). Land them at `/customers` and link from the Industries pages.

### Tier 2 — Trust and product-marketing parity

**5. Integrations directory with named partner pages.** Checkr's biggest visible advantage is its **200+ pre-built ATS/HRIS integrations in a searchable directory with per-partner pages**. Sterling names 12, HireRight 36, First Advantage 16. Rapid Hire's Integrations page exists but does not surface 20+ named partner cards with logos, an alphabetical filter, and a per-partner deep page (one paragraph each is enough). Even the appearance of 20+ named partners closes the perceived-scale gap with HireRight and Sterling overnight.

**6. Annual benchmark / trends report.** First Advantage publishes a "2026 Global Workforce Trends Report," HireRight runs a "Global Benchmark Report" + a quarterly "Hiring Confidence Series," Checkr publishes regulatory updates monthly. Rapid Hire should publish a single "2026 Background Screening Benchmark Report" — even if the dataset is modest, the artifact establishes thought leadership and works as a sales-enablement trade. Pair with the existing white paper library; gate behind email capture.

**7. ROI / TEI calculator.** Checkr's Forrester TEI calculator showing "169% ROI" is one of the highest-converting elements on their site, and GoodHire gates a "5 Steps To Getting Better ROI" guide. Rapid Hire already has a sophisticated **pricing** calculator on `/pricing`. Add a sibling **ROI calculator** on `/roi` (or as a tab on the pricing page) that converts inputs (annual hires, average bad-hire cost, current dispute rate, current turnaround) into projected savings + payback. The math is straightforward; the visible artifact is what closes deals.

**8. G2 / Gartner badges + named-logo trust band.** Checkr leads with G2 Market Leader and a "highest NPS in the industry" claim; GoodHire leads with SOC 2 + BBB; First Advantage leads with Newsweek 2025. Rapid Hire's Trust page already carries SOC 2 + PBSA + FCRA-aligned (strong) but the homepage hero badge band stops at "HIPAA + SOC 2 compliant." Add Gartner / G2 review-platform badges (apply if not yet listed), a "Trusted by 800+ HR teams" with logo wall, and a single quantified bragging stat (NPS or dispute rate) above the fold.

**9. Live chat or "talk to sales now" widget.** Three of five top sites (Sterling, Checkr, HireRight) run a live chat widget. Rapid Hire has none. A simple business-hours chat (Intercom, HubSpot, or even a Calendly "Book 15 min" pop-up) closes the gap with minimum engineering. Pair with the `/contact` form so the form remains the asynchronous fallback.

### Tier 3 — Content depth that sustains SEO leadership

**10. Industry pages with proof and benchmarks.** Rapid Hire already ships an `/industries` route with 6 verticals (§69). HireRight, Sterling, and First Advantage cover 8–17 industries each — Construction, Education, Energy & Utilities, Government, Hospitality, Manufacturing, Media, Nonprofit. Add at least Construction, Hospitality, and Education (high-volume hourly + credentialing). Each industry page should carry: a vertical-specific stat strip, a regulatory callout, a "what we run by default" check list (already the §69 pattern), and a vertical case study from recommendation 4.

**11. Glossary and FCRA Help Center.** Checkr's "Background check basics" hub and HireRight's "Candidate Help Center" are evergreen SEO surfaces. The §81–§82 resources work has built the long-form scaffolding; what's missing is a flat, alphabetical glossary at `/resources/glossary` covering 60–80 terms (FCRA, ICRAA, EEOC, OIG, SAM, MVR, Article 23-A, etc.). Each entry: 80–120 words + 2 internal links. Cheap to author, dense in long-tail SEO value.

**12. API documentation surface (or "Developers" link in nav).** Checkr's primary nav literally exposes a "Developers" item — a strong signal to technical buyers that the platform is API-first. If Rapid Hire's actual delivery is API + portal, expose `/developers` with a one-page API summary, Postman collection, and webhook spec. If it's portal-only today, skip this; if it's even partial, the visible nav entry is a credibility-by-existence signal.

**13. Resource type filtering.** Every top-5 Resource Center is filtered by **type** (Articles, Case Studies, Data Sheets, Guides, Infographics, News, Press Releases, Reports, Webinars, White Papers). The current `/resources` hub surfaces pillars by topic only. Add a single horizontal type-filter strip across the hub so "I just want a checklist" or "I just want a benchmark report" buyers can scan in one motion.

### Tier 4 — Strategic parity moves (consider, do not necessarily ship)

**14. Identity verification as a named pillar.** First Advantage and Sterling both lead with "Identity-First Services" / "Digital Identity Verification" as a top-level service category, separate from criminal/verifications. The market is moving in that direction post-pandemic. If Rapid Hire offers any identity workflow today (knowledge-based auth, document scan, biometric), promote it to a top-level service so buyers searching for "identity verification" find it.

**15. International capability page.** First Advantage (200+ countries), HireRight (200+), Sterling (global) all explicitly bid for global enterprise. If Rapid Hire serves global at all, an `/international` page with country-availability and GDPR/local-law notes captures that bucket. The §82 white paper "International Screening for U.S. Employers" already does the long-form heavy lifting; the page just needs to lift its highlights and put a CTA on top.

**16. Country selector in the header.** Sterling's header has a country selector before the candidate enters the portal. Skip this until international capability is real, but reserve the placement.

## What Rapid Hire is already doing better than the top 5

It is worth naming where the site is already ahead, so the redesign does not regress on its strengths.

The white paper library at 16 entries (post-§82) is **deeper than four of the five competitors' white-paper sections** in named-content depth. The 50-state background check matrix (`/resources/background-checks-by-state`), the 50-state cannabis-laws matrix, and the 13 hand-authored state pages are content GoodHire has a thinner version of and the other four do not have at all. The interactive pricing calculator at `/pricing` with package + add-on selection and live URL state is more sophisticated than any pricing surface I saw in the audit (Checkr and GoodHire both publish prices but do not let you build a quote interactively). The Trust page is well-executed with named-statute attestations.

The strategic implication: Rapid Hire's editorial / compliance content moat is real. The recommendations above are about closing the **product-marketing and conversion-infrastructure** gap so the content traffic actually converts.

## Suggested execution order

If we ship in two-recommendation increments, a defensible order is:

1. **§83a:** Per-check service pages + sample report PDF (recommendations 1–2)
2. **§83b:** Candidate page + case studies (recommendations 3–4)
3. **§83c:** Integrations directory + ROI calculator (recommendations 5, 7)
4. **§83d:** Trust band upgrade + live chat (recommendations 8–9)
5. **§83e:** Glossary + resource type filter + 3 more industry pages (recommendations 10, 11, 13)
6. Defer 6, 12, 14–16 until the above ship.

I can start on any of these immediately — recommendation 1 (per-check service pages) is the highest impact-per-effort because it both opens up a new SEO surface and gives the existing pricing calculator named anchors to link to.
