Texas has a reputation as one of the most employer-friendly states in the country — no statewide ban-the-box law, no salary-history ban, no general fair-chance mandate. That reputation lulls a lot of hiring teams into running one national screening policy across every Texas req.

The problem is that four separate rules quietly reshape what you can pull, keep, and act on:

- **DPS Crime Records access** — Chapter 411 governs the CCH system and the fingerprint-based FACT program, and sharply limits how you may reuse those records.
- **The chapter 20 seven-year cap** — for roles paying under $75,000, Texas bars reporting most criminal items older than seven years, stricter than the federal FCRA on convictions.
- **Orders of nondisclosure** — Texas keeps expanding record sealing, so a hit that surfaced two years ago may now be legally off-limits.
- **Austin's Fair Chance Hiring Ordinance** — a true ban-the-box mandate inside Austin city limits that a single statewide policy will almost always violate.

Our new guide maps each one, shows how they stack on top of the FCRA you already carry, and lays out a defensible Texas workflow end to end.
