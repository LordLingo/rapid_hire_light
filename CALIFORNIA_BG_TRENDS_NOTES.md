# California Background Check Trends 2025–2026 — Research Notes

## Big themes identified for SEO posts

1. **CRD Automated-Decision Systems regulations (effective Oct 1, 2025)** — codified at 2 CCR §11008+ — apply to employers using AI in employment screening, expand recordkeeping to 4 years, define ADS broadly, expand "agent" liability to vendors. Single most-searched 2026 CA HR-tech compliance topic.

2. **CRD CFCA enforcement ramp** — April 3, 2025 $100K settlement with CRST Expedited (trucking, Iowa-based). CRD now interpreting CFCA broadly, second-guessing employer final decisions even when process was followed. Growing class action exposure.

3. **LA County Fair Chance Ordinance for Employers (FCOE)** — effective Sept 3, 2024 — 5-employee threshold, applies regardless of employer location whenever the position will be performed in unincorporated LA County. Adds requirements beyond CFCA: explicit job-posting disclosures, conditional-offer letter requirements, longer adjudication timeline, private right of action.

4. **San Diego County Fair Chance Ordinance** — effective Oct 10, 2024 — same general structure as LA County's, applicable to unincorporated SD County. Adds another layer to CA's local/state patchwork.

5. **SB 731 "Clean Slate"** — by 2026, automatic sealing of most felony arrests with no conviction (3 years), most misdemeanor convictions (1 year after sentence), most felony convictions (4 years after sentence) — exclusions for serious/violent felonies, sex-offender registrants. Over 1 million records auto-sealed. Employers cannot consider sealed records.

6. **AB 2188 + SB 700 — Marijuana off-duty protection** — effective Jan 1, 2024 — non-psychoactive metabolite testing prohibited (urine, hair) for most employers. Federal exemptions (DOT, federal contractors). Active enforcement via FEHA / CRD.

7. **SB 1100 Driver's License requirement restrictions** — effective Jan 1, 2025 — illegal to require a driver's license in job postings unless 2-part test met (the position will require driving + alternative transportation can't be reasonably substituted). Backed by FEHA.

8. **AB 2095 (proposed 2026)** — would further restrict criminal history use; pending in CA Legislature.

9. **CCRAA + ICRAA dual-statute regime** — Connor v. First Student (Cal. Sup. Ct. 2018) confirmed both statutes apply to investigative reports. ICRAA "stand-alone" disclosure, 7-year limit on convictions. Existing post on rapid_hire covers ICRAA disclosure rules.

10. **Pay transparency SB 1162** — effective Jan 1, 2023, but enforcement matured 2025. Pay-scale disclosure on job postings (15+ employees) often crosses into background-check workflow when conditional offers are made.
